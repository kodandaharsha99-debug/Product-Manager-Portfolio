IIGovTech Initiative

The Strong Room — Closing a Security
Loophole at the Source

Security architecture · Compliance · Access control

The Strong Room module managed access to high-value government assets. The critical vulnerability was structural: the existing authentication flow had no defined protocol for unverified individuals. An unknown actor could reach the point of access with no mandatory secondary check in place. The system assumed good actors — a dangerous assumption in any high-stakes environment.

Product Intervention

Designed a Conditional Authentication Gate that activates when an individual cannot be verified through primary identification channels. Rather than allowing the process to continue or terminate without a record, the gate routes the event to a mandatory secondary path.

The "Other" Protocol

Introduced a Treasurer-level "Other" Identification Protocol: any unverified actor triggers a manual logging requirement — name, purpose, and timestamp — that must be completed and approved before access is granted. No approval without a record. No access without approval.

Outcome: 100% secure, fully auditable access. Every access event is attributable. The loophole is structurally closed, not patched.

IIIProduct Philosophy

How I Think About Products

Three principles that shape every decision from discovery to delivery

Accuracy as a Feature

In trust-based systems — payroll, security, compliance — accuracy isn't a quality bar. It's the product. A single discrepancy erodes the trust the entire system is built on. I design for correctness first.

Design for the Exception

The "Happy Path" is the easy part. Real products fail at the edges — the unverified actor, the missing record, the edge case nobody mapped. I treat exceptions as first-class requirements, not afterthoughts.

The Augmented PM

Leveraging AI and technical depth — SQL, code, system architecture — isn't a bonus skill. It's how I compress the distance between what stakeholders need and what engineering can build, faster and with less ambiguity.
